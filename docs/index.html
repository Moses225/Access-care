1 Who We Are

Morava Care LLC ("Morava," "we," "us," or "our") operates a mobile application and website (moravacare.com) designed to help SoonerCare, Medicaid, and uninsured patients in Oklahoma find and connect with healthcare providers. Morava is a healthcare technology platform — we are not a healthcare provider, medical practice, or insurance company.

This Privacy Policy applies to the Morava mobile app, the provider dashboard at dashboard.moravacare.com, and all related services (collectively, the "Service"). It governs how we collect, use, share, and protect your personal information, including Protected Health Information ("PHI") where applicable.

This Privacy Policy is incorporated by reference into our Terms of Service. In the event of a conflict between this Privacy Policy and our Terms of Service regarding the treatment of personal data or PHI, this Privacy Policy controls. For healthcare providers with whom we have executed a Business Associate Agreement ("BAA"), that BAA governs our handling of PHI on your behalf.

Questions? Contact us at support@moravacare.com or (432) 803-0136.

2 Key Definitions

  • "Personal Information" means any information that identifies or could reasonably identify you as an individual, including name, email address, and device identifiers.
  • "Protected Health Information" or "PHI" means individually identifiable health information created, received, maintained, or transmitted by Morava in connection with a covered healthcare provider, as defined under HIPAA (45 CFR § 160.103).
  • "Electronic PHI" or "ePHI" means PHI that is created, received, maintained, or transmitted in electronic form.
  • "Business Associate" means an entity that performs services on behalf of a covered entity that involve the use or disclosure of PHI, as defined under HIPAA (45 CFR § 160.103). Morava may act as a Business Associate when providing services to covered healthcare providers.
  • "Covered Entity" means a healthcare provider, health plan, or healthcare clearinghouse that transmits health information in electronic form in connection with HIPAA-covered transactions.
  • "De-identified Data" means data from which all 18 HIPAA identifiers have been removed in accordance with 45 CFR § 164.514(b), such that it cannot reasonably be used to identify an individual.

4 How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect only for the following purposes:

  • Create, authenticate, and manage your Morava account.
  • Connect you with healthcare providers who accept your insurance type.
  • Process and track appointment requests submitted through the Service.
  • Transmit your name, contact information, and relevant health profile details to providers with whom you book an appointment, solely to facilitate that appointment.
  • Send appointment confirmation, status update, and reminder notifications (push via Firebase; SMS via Twilio — see Section 5 regarding current Twilio BAA status).
  • Display your voucher eligibility if you are among Morava's first 100 founding members.
  • Improve app features and fix technical issues using aggregated, anonymized analytics.
  • Respond to your support requests and privacy inquiries.
  • Comply with legal obligations, including HIPAA, HITECH, COPPA, and applicable Oklahoma law.
  • Detect, investigate, and prevent fraud, security incidents, and other illegal activity.
🚫

Morava does not display advertisements and has no plans to do so. This is a founding principle of Morava and will not change.

4.1 Minimum Necessary Standard

Consistent with HIPAA's minimum necessary standard (45 CFR § 164.502(b)), Morava limits its use and disclosure of PHI to the minimum amount necessary to accomplish the intended purpose. We do not access, process, or transmit more PHI than is required for the specific function being performed.

4.2 De-identification

Where we use data for analytics, product improvement, or research, we apply de-identification methods consistent with 45 CFR § 164.514(b) to ensure that individuals cannot reasonably be identified. De-identified data is not subject to HIPAA restrictions and may be used to improve the Service.

6 Data Storage and Security

6.1 Infrastructure

Your data is stored on Google Firebase (Firestore and Firebase Authentication), a secure cloud platform operated by Google LLC under a HIPAA Business Associate Agreement with Morava. All data is encrypted in transit using TLS 1.3 and at rest using AES-256 encryption.

6.2 HIPAA Security Rule Compliance

Morava maintains a comprehensive information security program aligned with HIPAA's Security Rule (45 CFR Part 164, Subpart C), including:

  • Administrative safeguards: Designated Security Officer, workforce HIPAA training, access management policies, risk assessment and risk management program, contingency planning.
  • Physical safeguards: Hosted on Google's SOC 2-certified infrastructure with physical access controls. No PHI is stored on employee devices.
  • Technical safeguards: Encryption of ePHI in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256); unique user authentication; automatic logoff; audit controls and immutable audit logs; transmission security.

6.3 Access Controls

  • Patients can only read and write their own records — no cross-patient data access is permitted.
  • Providers can only see appointment requests submitted specifically to them.
  • Audit logs are write-only and immutable — no user, including administrators, can delete or alter audit entries.
  • Analytics events are write-only for authenticated users — no patient can read other patients' events.
  • All provider actions (confirm, decline, reschedule bookings) verify provider identity before execution.
  • Administrative access to PHI is limited on a minimum-necessary basis to authorized Morava personnel only.

6.4 Risk Assessments

Morava conducts periodic risk assessments of its systems, infrastructure, and processes that involve PHI, consistent with HIPAA Security Rule requirements (45 CFR § 164.308(a)(1)). Identified risks are tracked and mitigated according to documented remediation plans.

6.5 Crash Reporting (Sentry)

We use Sentry for crash monitoring under a signed BAA (executed May 2026). Before any error data is transmitted to Sentry, the app automatically strips all PII fields including names, emails, phone numbers, insurance information, and appointment details. Stack traces are truncated in production to prevent data leakage through file paths.

6.6 Security Limitations

Despite these measures, no electronic transmission or storage system is 100% secure. You use the Service at your own risk. You are responsible for maintaining the security of your account credentials and notifying us immediately of any suspected unauthorized access at support@moravacare.com.

7 Children's Privacy (COPPA)

🛡️ Users Under 13 Are Not Permitted

Morava is not directed to children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If we learn we have inadvertently collected such information, we will delete it within 72 hours.

Morava requires all users to provide their birth year during account creation. If a user indicates they are under 13, account creation is blocked and they are directed to contact us with a parent or guardian.

Birth year is collected solely for COPPA age verification. It is stored in your user record but is never used for profiling, analytics segmentation, advertising, or any purpose other than verifying that you are 13 or older.

For users aged 13 to 17: A parent or legal guardian may contact us at support@moravacare.com to: (a) review the minor's account information; (b) request correction of inaccurate data; or (c) request deletion of the minor's account and all associated data. We will respond to such requests within 72 hours.

If you believe a child under 13 has created an account or submitted personal information, please contact us immediately at support@moravacare.com. We will delete the account and all associated data within 72 hours of verification.

8 Your Rights and Choices

8.1 General Privacy Rights

  • Right to Access: Email support@moravacare.com to request a copy of all personal information we hold about you. We will provide a portable copy within 30 days.
  • Right to Correct: Update your name, email, and insurance information directly in the app under Profile → Edit Profile, or by contacting us.
  • Right to Delete: Request full deletion of your account and all associated personal data. We will complete deletion within 30 days and confirm by email. See Section 10 for retention exceptions.
  • Right to Restrict Processing: Request that we restrict certain uses of your information while a correction or objection is pending.
  • Right to Data Portability: Request your personal data in a structured, machine-readable format.

8.2 HIPAA-Specific Rights

To the extent Morava holds PHI on behalf of a covered entity healthcare provider, you may have the following rights, which must be exercised through the applicable provider:

  • Right of Access to PHI (45 CFR § 164.524): Request a copy of your PHI held by your provider. Morava will cooperate with providers to fulfill these requests.
  • Right to Amendment (45 CFR § 164.526): Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete PHI in your records.
  • Right to an Accounting of Disclosures (45 CFR § 164.528): Request a list of disclosures of your PHI other than for treatment, payment, or healthcare operations purposes.
  • Right to Restrict (45 CFR § 164.522): Request restrictions on certain uses and disclosures of your PHI. Note: providers are not required to agree to all restrictions.
  • Right to Confidential Communications (45 CFR § 164.522(b)): Request that your PHI be communicated by alternative means or at an alternative location.
  • Right to File a Complaint: If you believe your HIPAA privacy rights have been violated, you may file a complaint with the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services Office for Civil Rights (OCR) at www.hhs.gov/ocr or 1-800-368-1019. Morava will not retaliate against you for filing a complaint.

8.3 Communication Preferences

  • Push notifications: Manage in your device's notification settings or in the Morava app settings.
  • SMS notifications: Reply STOP to any SMS from Morava at any time to opt out. (SMS is currently paused — see Section 5.2.)
  • Email notifications: Follow the unsubscribe link in any Morava email, or contact support@moravacare.com.

8.4 Location

You can deny location permission at any time in your device settings. Location access is optional and is never required to use the core features of the Service. When location is used, it is processed in-session only and is not stored to a database.

8.5 Analytics

Morava's analytics are collected internally and are never shared with advertising networks, data brokers, or any third party. There is no behavioral advertising opt-out required because we do not engage in behavioral advertising.

8.6 How to Exercise Your Rights

To exercise any of the rights described above, contact us at support@moravacare.com, (432) 803-0136, or Morava Care LLC, 14556 N Pennsylvania Ave, Building B Apt 305, Oklahoma City, OK 73134. We will respond to all privacy rights requests within 30 days. For requests involving children's data, we respond within 72 hours. We may need to verify your identity before processing certain requests to protect against unauthorized access.

9 Oklahoma Privacy Rights

In addition to your HIPAA rights, Oklahoma residents may have rights under the Oklahoma Health Records Act (63 O.S. § 1-501 et seq.) and the Oklahoma Consumer Privacy Act ("OCPA"), where applicable. These may include:

  • The right to access your health records maintained by your healthcare provider.
  • The right to obtain copies of your health records within the timeframes required by Oklahoma law.
  • Rights applicable to SoonerCare and Medicaid recipients under Oklahoma Health Care Authority regulations.

To exercise any Oklahoma-specific rights, contact us at support@moravacare.com. We will direct your request appropriately and cooperate with applicable regulatory authorities.

11 Security Incident and Breach Notification

Morava maintains comprehensive safeguards to protect your information. In the event of a data security incident involving your Protected Health Information (PHI), Morava will comply with HIPAA's Breach Notification Rule (45 CFR §§ 164.400–164.414):

  • Promptly investigate and assess the scope of any unauthorized access or disclosure.
  • Notify affected individuals within 60 days of discovering a breach, unless a law enforcement exception applies.
  • Notify the applicable covered entity healthcare provider without unreasonable delay.
  • Notify the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) as required by HIPAA.
  • For breaches affecting 500 or more individuals in Oklahoma, provide notice to prominent media outlets in the state, as required by HIPAA.
  • Provide breach notices that include: a description of what occurred, the types of information involved, steps you can take to protect yourself, what Morava is doing to investigate and prevent recurrence, and contact information for follow-up questions.

We will provide notification by email (to the address associated with your account) or, if email is unavailable, by prominent notice on moravacare.com and in the app.

⚠️

Report a Suspected Security Incident. If you believe your account or health information may have been compromised, contact us immediately at support@moravacare.com or (432) 803-0136. You may also report a HIPAA violation to HHS OCR at www.hhs.gov/ocr or 1-800-368-1019.

12 Founding Member Voucher Program

Morava's founding member voucher program tracks the total number of signups using an atomic counter. If you are among the first 100 users to create an account, your user record is flagged with voucherEligible: true. This flag:

  • Is visible only to you and Morava's authorized administrative team.
  • Is never shared with providers, third parties, or advertising partners.
  • Is used solely to display the free Meet & Greet badge in the app.
  • Changes to voucherUsed: true only after a provider confirms a Meet & Greet appointment.

Voucher eligibility data is treated as personal information under this Privacy Policy and is deleted when you delete your account.
